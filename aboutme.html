My Blurb

In 2015 I was near to slamming a tablet computer to the ground. I was frustrated. The voices in my head had been nagging me constantly with negativity for 18 months. They were awful and I realized I had to do something to help myself. I didn't know what schizophrenia was, but this was the first thing I looked at. Almost a decade later I've learned a bit. Before 2015 I spent my 33rd birthday in a psychiatric hospital with lime green walls.
My preferred form of therapy has been bibliotherapy as my life has been sitting in a room and reading. But there is more to life. There is emotion. I don't really feel them yet, but I know they exist. I'm interested in knowlege, I had an artistic period in my twenties. I obtained a bachelors degree. I have few friends because no one really likes people who can't help themselves. I don't blame others, this is just how life is. We're in a struggle to survive and our way of life is competition. I'm grateful to many people that make up this city, this state, and this nation in which I live.
