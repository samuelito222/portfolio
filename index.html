Would Schizophrenia by Any Other Name Smell as Much?

Here is my portfolio website. My intent is to practice job skills. For content I'm using what I know. So please take everything with plenty of grains of salt. Preferrably pepper because of limits on sodium intake in a healthy diet and because pepper is just awesome!

Symptoms

I don't know much about my interior stuff This is the meaning of anosognosia. Basically the word means a lack of insight. Kinda seems huffy for a psychiatrist to invent a term that means he knows more about you than you do, but it's an actual thing. I didn't know much about what I've experienced before I really thought about my life. Everybody knows what they've been through, This took a lot of time. I would sit stuck asking myself how does one think about oneself. A therapist of mine asked me to list twenty adjectives that described myself. This was quite tough. But over time (years for me) I've gained a poco bit of insight.

I can't sit still. For me, part of this, is my fight or flight response is always going off. The answer for me is to find something I like to do and can put out of mind other stuff. Coding a web page is difficult. I get bothered and need to take breaks, but I also like the results when a web page works. I listen to music. I have my water jug close by and a tidbit of snacks. Sitting still is relative. I haven't solved this one yet, but I hope what I'm sharing helps.

How to feel if you can't feel. First off we must listen to Phil Collins singing I can feel it in the air tonight. Then begin with simple emotions. I started with shame. I've done some shameful things. No excuses being sick, some things I've done have been shameful. I can feel sad, but it's easier to cry for someone else. Did something happen in high school and someone got hurt (emotionally) because of your involvement? Cry for them! Don't be a robot Then move on to anger. I had to learn about disgust. You can imagine some of my behavior if I had no sense of disgust! Don't write that they don't have to know everything!

Things take time to accomplish. This requires organization. On a pleasant stay in a psychiatric hospital. Our group had a visit and listened to a presentation from some volunteers from NAMI. One mentioned he was planning on three consecutive bachelor degrees. I am in a similar situation as to thinking my interests are too expansive. How do I accomplish them all. For one, I got a planner and take copious notes. One would think my planner, the stickers, the post-it notes; are all a jumble, and they are. But I'm improving on my organization. Another tool is reduction. Find out what you can do without. I thought electronics were the neatest thing. But on second thought I can do without playing with a soltering iron and coding on a raspberry pi.

How to even define such a thing as a detachment from reality? I watch a schizophrenia vlogger who is always asking herself what is real? Maybe it's a poor choice of question. You only live so long. So ask yourself better questions. Like. What could I do to make that man/woman like me. What could I do to be happier. What could I do to like myself better. What does he think about that. What type of people do I like. I'm sure you can think of better questions but the point is just not to ask such a question that questions reality.

Our brains have a reward strategy. You get the feel good chemical for achievement. It's just how the brain works. So if you're not rowing in the river. You're not that happy with yourself. What I don't know everything, these are metaphors!!!
