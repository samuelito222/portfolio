Resources
  • Goodwill

    After three attempts I got a job here. I worked here for two and a half years. They were always looking for new people to hire. They have plenty of work to do. I learned some important knowledge about business here.

  • NAMI

    I had attended a NAMI group in another city after being recommended by a psychiatrist. Before the pandemic they had in person group therapy sessions twice a month. This was very helpful to be in a group of people struggling with similar issues.

  • Integral Care

    I used the local mental health services in a few cities. they have plenty of resources, but they are not going to get you a job and get you housing. It was just so hard to learn to be my own best advocate. And the reason why I didn't already know this was so hard to learn. Alas.

  • Community Radio

    I listen to several shows on this station which shares a signal with the local university radio station. Both offer a rather abundant selection of top notch programming. It's never the greatest hits all the time. It's always interesting.

  • The Arc of the Capital

    I just found out about this place by listening to a radio show. I haven't used any services, but another resource is another resource, tautalogically speaking.

  • Texas Workforce Commission: Vocational Rehabilitation Services, formally DARS

    I used this service twice. With my counselors I learned that it is a job to get a job. I really went into this thinking that they would just place me somewhere. No. You have to work to show you can work. I did get enrolled in a Accounting/Bookkeeping course and learned Quickbooks. But the lack of self knowledge and not knowing what was good for me and what I desired to do. A correct fit if you will, hindered our progress.

  • Supplemental Security Income

    Wow. To have some steady money. This was a game changer to be able to have a monthly income of a few hundred. It's not enough to live on. At first I thought I should just live simply and use this for everything. But as I write elsewhere life is about work. And the more enjoyable work you can place yourself into the higher quality of life you can have. I had to learn this, I didn't know.

  • Therapists and Psychiatrists

    A therapy professional nor a psychiatrist is not going to cure you. I wanted more help than they could give. Alas, I did not know that i had to help myself. In hindsight, they were all pleasant to talk to. Schizophrenia is a rather tough nut to crack and they all did help. I am grateful of their time with me.

  • Local clubs for people with mental illness

    I attended a local art club for the disabled and they had some neat art supplies. This was good for me because my social skills were subpar for a put put golf course.

  • Community Colleges

    Community college is so cool. I really enjoyed my Accounting/Bookkeeping course. The instructor had industry experience and was on point. She knew her stuff and I enjoyed learning from her. The learning assistant gave me a hard time. I couldn't tell her to lay off, because I didn't want to talk about having schizophrenia, but if someone doesn't like you or gives you a hard time, it just sharpens you up and prepares you for better things.
